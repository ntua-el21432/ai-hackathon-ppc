## What will change in electricity tariffs as of 01.01.2024 and why?
As of January 1st, 2024, the new electricity supply tariffs for low-voltage consumers will be applied. Among the available tariffs, a Special Tariff is to be determined by the State, in which customers will automatically be included on 1 January 2024 (default tariff).
Customers who have already concluded a fixed-term period contract for a fixed-rate product will not automatically switch to the Special Tariff, for as long as their contract is in force.
Customers have the right to object and not switch to the Special Tariff until 31 January 2024. This means that customers can choose to stay on the tariff they already have or any other tariff they wish among those available.

## New color-coded electricity tariffs
Four distinct Electricity Product categories are established by the State, to be offered by all suppliers without exception.
- **Fixed-rate Products (Blue Label):** Fixed supply price (€/kWh) throughout the duration of the contract period.
- **Variable-rate Products (Yellow Label):** Electricity products with variable supply pricing (€/kWh) varying based on the fluctuations in the wholesale electricity market.
- **Dynamic-rate Products (Orange Label):** Tariffs varying based on the electricity wholesale market prices within a single day. These tariffs are available on the condition that the customer has a smart meter installed.
- **Special Tariff (Green Label):** The Final Price will be announced by the supplier by the first day of the month of application, i.e. customers will know by the 1st day of each month the price they will pay for the respective month of consumption. All customers will switch automatically to the Special Tariff (default tariff) on January 1st, 2024.

## Why did I switch to the Special Tariff?
According to the new legislation passed by the State and aiming at ensuring the smooth transition of the market, all Suppliers are required to create a Special Tariff, to which all customers will switch as of 01.01.2024. Customers will be informed in detail by letter to be sent by the Supplier with whom they currently have a supply contract.

## Do I have to stay on the Special Tariff?
No, it is not mandatory. In case you decide that you do not want to stay on the Special Tariff you have the right to object and select the product that best suits your needs.
Call us at 800-500-7000 or visit a PPC Branch from 01.12.2023 to 31.01.2024 to state your objection to switching to the Special Tariff. In case you do not wish to switch to the Special Tariff you have the right to either stay on your current product or choose a new electricity supply product. In any case, you will be informed of the conditions applied to the product that you will select.

## How are the charges for the tariff lists of the special products Γ21 Business, Γ23 Business, Agricultural, Public Lighting from 01.01.2024 shaped by the new reality?
The Supply Charge for the above products will result from a fixed component (Basic Supply Price) and a variable component (Fluctuation Mechanism) and will be reflected in the electricity bill as a single price (Final Supply Price). The final price of each month will be posted on our website www.dei.gr by the 1st day of each month of consumption and will be accompanied by a detailed file with information on how to verify the price.
In particular, in the Special Tariffs, the charges consist of:
1. The monthly fee
2. The Final Supply Charge, which will include the Basic Supply Charge and the Fluctuation Mechanism

## What applies to the Special tariffs Γ21 Business, Γ23 Business, Agricultural and Public Lighting from 01.01.2025?
The Special Terms, which were applicable and valid on 01.12.2024, will continue to apply beginning 01.01.2025.

## How will the Supply Charge be reflected on electricity bills included in the Special Tariff?
Suppliers are required to display in a uniform manner the Final Supply Price per billing month on the electricity bills included in the Special Tariff.

## How frequently may the Special Tariffs terms change?
- **Basic Supply Charge:** The Basic Supply Charge remains stable for at least six (6) months before it can be changed. Customers will be notified two (2) months in advance of any changes through personalized alerts and the PPC website.
- **Fixed fee:** The monthly fixed fee may vary from month to month. Customers will be notified on the 1st day of the month of consumption via an announcement on the PPC website, as well as a notice on the first electricity bill issued following the change.
- **Discount on the Basic Supply Charge:** The discount may vary from month to month. Customers will be notified on the 1st day of the month of consumption via an announcement on the PPC website, as well as a notice on the first electricity bill issued following the change.
- **Fluctuation Mechanism parameters (Fluctuation Factors and Limits):** The Fluctuation Mechanism parameters remain stable for at least three (3) months. Customers will be notified one (1) month before the new parameters are implemented via an announcement on the PPC website, as well as a notice on the first electricity bill issued following the change.

## Can I switch my tariff or supplier immediately and without an exit fee?
Yes, you can. You may switch supplier or supply tariff at any time, and the supplier is not entitled to any compensation due to early withdrawal of the customer, except in cases of termination of a fixed-rate contract.

## Do you no longer offer fixed-rate tariffs?
We remain consistent with our strategy of offering fixed tariffs, which so far have created a safety net for over 600,000 customers. We will always stand by our customers, truly offering you the best choice.

## How does the fluctuation mechanism work?
In variable-rate products, there is a variable component that depends on the energy cost in the market (MCP) and is regulated by the fluctuation mechanism.

## What is the Market Clearing Price (TEA in Greek)?
The MCP is the market clearing price which is published daily on an hourly basis on the website of the Hellenic Exchange. The MCP is used to calculate the variable component of the charge, is not determined by the supplier, and is common for the entire market. The fluctuation mechanism and the subsequent eventual credit or debit to your account are determined based on the MCP.
When and how is the mechanism activated?
- If the MCP is outside of the range, the amount resulting from the fluctuation mechanism is reflected on the bill
- Zero charge, when the MCP variable is between the lower and upper limit
