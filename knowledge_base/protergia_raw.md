Is there a risk of my power supply being discontinued during the Supplier change procedure?

No. Your business and your home will not be left without electricity – not for a moment. The Supplier change transaction is a purely commercial one. The supply of electricity is guaranteed by the Hellenic Electricity Distribution Network Operator (DEDDHE).

What is the Supplier of Last Resort?

The Supplier of Last Resort is obliged to supply electricity to customers who are not represented by a supplier for reasons for which their most recent supplier is to blame or as a result of events within the sphere of control of their most recent supplier. By Decision of the Greek Regulatory Authority for Energy (RAE), the Supplier of Last Resort is determined to be the Supplier with the largest market share per category of customers (currently the PPC). Migration to the service of last resort takes place automatically as far as the customer is concerned.

What is the Universal Service Provider?

The Universal Service Provider is obliged to supply electricity to small customers across the entire Greek territory, provided that one of the following apply: (a) The above candidate customers have failed to exercise their right to freely select a supplier; (b) The above candidate customers are unable to find a supplier in the liberalised market on their present based on their current commercial terms. Migration to the universal service takes place automatically as far as the customer is concerned.

### Bill and Charges

How often will I receive a bill?

Protergia electricity bills are issued on a monthly basis and belong to one of the following types: ESTIMATED -> Based on an estimated consumption level. ACTUAL -> Based on the consumption measured and certified by the Distribution Network Operator.. ACTUAL – CUSTOMER READING -> Based on the “Registration of Meter Readings” service.

How is the charge worked out in an ACTUAL bill if the measurement of consumption is taken at a time interval that is longer or shorter than four months?

If for any reason the consumption is measured at a time interval that is longer or shorter than four months, then the consumption charged is adjusted relative to the four-month period by applying the “billing period adjustment factor” (actual consumption days / 120 days).

What costs are included in the Competitive Charges?

Competitive charges comprise the following: The charge for the supply of power under the Day Ahead Scheduling mechanism, the charges of the Uplift Accounts (UA-2, UA-3), charges for losses in the Hellenic Electricity Distribution Network, the charge based on the weighted average production cost of conventional thermal plants (Charged to the Special Account of article 143 of Law 4001/2011) and the costs of the Transitional Capacity Assurance Mechanism (MMDEI), as defined in the Electricity Transaction Code (Decision 56/2012 of the Regulatory Authority for Energy, published in Government Gazette issue 104/B/2012) and determined in accordance with the laws in force.

What other charges will I see in my bill?

In addition to your electricity consumption, your bill will also include Regulated Charges and the corresponding taxes and duties under the laws in force.

What are Regulated Charges?

Regulated charges are the same for all consumers, depending on the particular tariff category and irrespective of the electricity supplier selected. They comprise the charges levied on all consumers who use the electricity infrastructures (i.e. the Hellenic Electricity Transmission System and the Hellenic Electricity Distribution Network, the charges for the provision of Services of General Interest and the Special Duty for the Reduction of Air Pollutant Emissions).

What are the Other Third Party Charges?

The following charges are levied on all consumers irrespective of the electricity supplier selected, are collected via the payment of electricity bills and are remitted to the State: (a) Excise Duty – EFK (Law 2960/01). The Excise Duty is charged only in ACTUAL bills, is calculated based on the level of electricity consumption and is subject to VAT. (b) Special Customs Rights Duty 5‰ – DETE (Law 2093/1992). DETE is charged in the ESTIMATED and ACTUAL bills and is calculated on the charge for the consumption of electricity augmented by the Excise Duty (EFK) charge. It is not subject to a VAT charge. (c) Municipal Duties – Municipal Taxes – Real Estate Duty (Law 25/1975, Law 2130/1993). Municipal Duties (DT), Municipal Taxes (DF) and the Real Estate Duty (TAP) are charged only in ACTUAL bills and are calculated based on the surface (in sq.m.) of the property which is supplied with power.

### Net Metering questions

What is net metering (self-generation with energy offset)?

As instituted with Ministerial Decision ΑΠΕΗΛ/Α/Φ1/οικ.24461 (Government Gazette B/3583/2014), and in accordance with MD ΥΠΕΝ/ΔΑΠΕΕΚ/15084/382, (GovGaz B/759/2019), the development of photovoltaic systems by self-generators (“prosumers”) involves the installation of fixed photovoltaic systems to meet consumers’ own need for electricity and the application of an energy offset.  
  
Energy offset, or net metering, means that the energy generated by a photovoltaic system is offset against the energy consumed at the premises of the self-generator, and it is calculated on a three-year basis. In net metering, it is not necessary for the energy generated to coincide with the energy consumed.

Who can install a power generation system using net metering?

Those entitled to install such a system are natural persons and legal persons governed by public or private law who either own the space where the system is to be installed, or they have lawful access to it (e.g. through a lease) and have obtained written consent from the owner of the site.  
Where the generation system is in a common-use or jointly owned part of a building, one or more systems may be installed, each of which corresponds to just one meter. Those entitled to install a net metering system in this case are the owners of the horizontal properties (flats) or those who have lawful use of them, once they have been granted use of the common-use or jointly owned space or section of the space by the other co-owners.

What are the main terms and conditions for including a consumer in a net metering scheme?

1. There must an active and permanent power supply in the name of the self-generator which supplies the electrical installation.
2. The photovoltaic system must correspond to one electricity meter, which is the meter for the installation it supplies.
3. The system must be installed at the same or an adjacent site to the electrical installation that it corresponds to.
4. The applicant must have lawful use of the site where the system is to be installed.
5. The applicant must have paid all electricity bills issued by the relevant suppliers or have made arrangements to settle any outstanding debts.
6. The self-generator’s supply must not be a beneficiary of the Environmental Residential Tariff (MD ΥΠΕΝ/ΥΠΡΓ/26359/4101 – GovGaz B/1936/30.5.2018).

What is the duration of the net metering agreement?

The net metering agreement signed between the provider and the self-generator is for a 25-year term, starting from the date the connection with the photovoltaic system is activated.

In the event of a change in provider after the system’s activation, the net metering agreement with the previous provider is automatically terminated and a new net metering agreement is concluded between the self-generator and the new provider for the balance of the 25-year period.

Similarly, if the contracting customer of the electrical installation changes, a new net metering agreement is concluded for the remaining time period.

When are meter readings taken and when is net metering applied to generated and consumed energy?

Metering of electricity generated by the energy system and the incoming and outgoing energy from and to the grid is undertaken at the same time by the Hellenic Electricity Distribution Network Operator (DEDDIE), during the current metering cycle which applies to the self-generator’s electrical installation.

The provider applies net metering on the “actual” electricity bills, based on the consumption measured by DEDDIE, according to the metering cycle in effect. In the event that it is not possible to obtain a meter reading on the scheduled metering date, net metering will be applied when the next regular metering takes place.

How are regulated charges applied?

Regulated charges are calculated as follows:

1. The special duty for greenhouse gas emissions reduction (ETMEAR) is calculated based on the absorbed energy and the corresponding unit charge.
2. The variable part of the infrastructure use charges and network use charges (energy charges) is calculated based on the absorbed energy and the corresponding unit charge.

Charges for Services of General Interest are calculated based on the total energy consumption and the corresponding unit charge. If tariffs from different time zones are involved, the total produced and guaranteed energy will be used to calculate the charges for general interest services corresponding only to the normal charge zone (day use charges).

### We are here to answer all your questions!

Will my energy provider change?

Yes, your new provider will be **Protergia**, a part of **METLEN Energy & Metals S.A.**, the largest private energy company in Greece. It operates in the supply of electricity and natural gas, offering modern and reliable services.

Will there be any changes to my electricity meter?

Your meter will remain the same. However, after the merger, your supply ID number will change by increasing the last digit. For example, from 1-12345678-01 to 1-12345678-02.

Additionally, based on this number, a corresponding **12-digit personal identification code** will be created, which you can use at customer service points with Protergia.

More specifically, the previously 11-digit Supply Number will have one additional digit at the end, e.g., 1-12345678-02 0. This number will appear on your bill and will be requested for customer service.

Will the Electronic Delivery Point ID Code (ΗΚΑΣΠ) for natural gas change?

No, the **natural gas** **ΗΚΑΣΠ** number is unique and **will not change**.

What changes in my contract?

According to the recent letter you received, starting **June 2025**, your contract will be transferred to Protergia, who will be your new electricity and/or natural gas provider. Your pricing terms will follow the **Protergia tariff** described in your personalized letter, based on your customer category.

What changes in the electricity bill charges?

**A) Supply Charge Calculations**: The way variable rate electricity tariffs are calculated per customer category will change, and this will apply for 12 months starting in April. You can learn more from the following links:

* + [Protergia Value Lite](/en/home/electric-energy/protergia-value-lite/ "Protergia Value Lite")
  + [Protergia Business 1 Value Simple](/en/business/electricity-energy-for-business/protergia-business-1-value-simple/ "Protergia Business 1 Value Simple")
  + [Protergia Business 2 Value Simple](/en/business/electricity-energy-for-business/protergia-business-2-value-simple/ "Protergia Business 2 Value Simple")
  + [Protergia Business 3 Value Simple](/en/business/electricity-energy-for-business/protergia-business-3-value-simple/ "Protergia Business 3 Value Simple")

**B) Estimation Method for Provisional Bills**: Provisional (estimated) bills will be issued when monthly certified meter readings by the DEDDHE (grid operator) are not available.

If readings are available, the average of those will be used. If not, consumption will be estimated based on:

1. Historical meter data during your current contract, or
2. Average consumption of similar customers (same size and usage type).

The **lower of the two** will be used for billing.  
Estimated bills are reconciled with an actual reading-based bill approximately every **4 months**.

*Note*: **[Regulated Charges](https://www.protergia.gr/en/home/electricity-energy/calculation-of-regulated-electricity-tariffs/)** and **[Third-Party Charges](https://www.protergia.gr/en/home/electricity-energy/calculation-of-regulated-electricity-tariffs/)** remain unchanged and apply to all suppliers under existing laws.

What changes in the gas bill charges?

**A) Supply Charge Calculations**: The method of calculating natural gas variable rate tariffs per customer category changes starting April, valid for 12 months. Check these links:

* + [Protergia Gas Residential](/en/home/natural-gas/stand-alone-connection/ "Stand-alone connection")
  + [Protergia Gas Shared/Common](/en/home/natural-gas/natural-gas-tariffs-communal/ "Natural Gas tariffs - Communal")
  + [Protergia Gas Business](/en/business/natural-gas/ "Natural Gas")

**B) Estimation Method for Provisional Bills**: Consumption estimates will be based on:

* The same period last year (based on meter readings),
* The previous billing period,
* Estimated annual consumption and installed load based on typical usage patterns from the Distribution Network Code.

*Note*: Regulated fees, taxes, and surcharges will still be applied uniformly to all suppliers under current law.
